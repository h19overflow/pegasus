## 1. Quick Start

**Don't know where to begin? Call 211.**

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Phone | Dial **2-1-1** (or 888-421-1266) — 24/7, free, confidential |
| Text | Send your ZIP code to **898-211** |
| TTY | Dial **7-1-1** |
| Online | [211connectsalabama.org](https://211connectsalabama.org) |

A trained operator assesses your needs and connects you to specific local programs. 211 covers food, housing, utilities, healthcare, employment, legal aid, transportation, and more.

**Local 211 operator:** HandsOn River Region — (334) 264-3335, 1537 Jean Street, Montgomery, AL 36107.

### For AI Agents

When a user says "I need help" without specifying what kind, start by asking which category they need. If they're unsure, recommend calling 211 or walk them through each section below to identify what applies.

## 2. Alabama Medicaid

> **Critical note:** Alabama has NOT expanded Medicaid under the ACA. Childless adults ages 19–64 are ineligible regardless of income. They should check Healthcare.gov for Marketplace plans.

### Programs Available

| Program | Who Qualifies | Income Limit |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Children (under 19) | Low-income families | 146% FPL |
| ALL Kids (CHIP) | Children above Medicaid limit | Up to 317% FPL |
| Pregnant Women | Prenatal through 12 months postpartum | 146% FPL |
| Parents/Caretakers | With dependent children under 19 | 18% FPL (extremely low) |
| Elderly/Blind/Disabled | Age 65+, blind, or disabled | SSI level ($987/month individual) |
| Nursing Home / Long-Term Care | Need nursing facility level of care | $2,901/month cap |
| Plan First (Family Planning) | Females 19–55, Males 21+ | 146% FPL |
| Medicare Savings (QMB) | Medicare beneficiaries | $1,325/month individual |

### Income Limits — Pregnant Women, Children, Plan First (146% FPL)

|-------------|---------|--------|
| 1 | $1,905 | $22,860 |
| 2 | $2,574 | $30,888 |
| 3 | $3,243 | $38,916 |

### Income Limits — Parents/Caretaker Relatives (~18% FPL)

| Family Size | Monthly | Annual |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| 1 | $235 | $2,820 |

| 4 | $483 | $5,796 |

### How to Apply — Step by Step

1. **Gather documents:** ID (birth certificate, passport, or driver's license), Social Security numbers for all household members, last 4 pay stubs, proof of Alabama residency
2. **Choose application method:**
   - **Children/Pregnant Women online:** https://insurealabama.adph.state.al.us/
   - **Elderly/Disabled online:** https://eanddapplication.medicaid.alabama.gov/
   - **Phone:** 1-800-362-1504 (Mon–Fri, 8am–4:30pm)
   - **Mail:** P.O. Box 304500, Montgomery, AL 36130-4500
   - **In person:** Visit a Medicaid District Office
3. **Submit application** with all verification documents
4. **Attend interview** (phone or in-person, typically scheduled within 10 days)
5. **Wait for processing:** Standard 45 days, disability-related 90 days
6. **Receive decision by mail.** If approved, Medicaid card arrives in 10–14 days
7. **Benefits are retroactive** up to 3 months if you were eligible

### Required Documents

- Birth certificate, passport, or driver's license
- Social Security numbers for all household members
- Last 4 pay stubs or income verification
- SSI/SSD award letter (if applicable)
- Bank statements (for programs with asset tests)
- Proof of Alabama residency
- Medicare card (if applicable)

### Key Contacts

| Contact | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Recipient Call Center | **1-800-362-1504** (Mon–Fri, 8am–4:30pm) |
| ALL Kids Hotline | **888-373-5437** |
| Main Office | 501 Dexter Avenue, Montgomery, AL 36103 |
| Email | Apply@medicaid.alabama.gov |
| Website | https://medicaid.alabama.gov |

### For AI Agents

To determine Medicaid eligibility: ask for (1) age, (2) pregnancy status, (3) disability status, (4) number of people in household, (5) monthly income. Compare against the tables above. If the user is a childless adult 19–64, they do NOT qualify — redirect to Healthcare.gov marketplace plans.

## 3. SNAP / Food Assistance

> Over 750,000 Alabama residents receive SNAP. Benefits are loaded monthly onto an EBT card accepted at 250,000+ retail locations.

### Eligibility

| Family Size | Monthly Gross Income Limit (130% FPL) | Max Monthly Benefit |
|-------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | $1,644 | $292 |
| 2 | $2,229 | — |
| 3 | $2,822 | — |
| 4 | $3,415 | — |
| 5 | $4,008 | — |
| 6 | $4,601 | — |
| 7 | $5,194 | — |
| 8 | $5,787 | $1,759 |

**Additional rules:**
- Must be a U.S. citizen or qualified non-citizen
- Must reside in Alabama
- Asset limit: $4,500 for elderly/disabled households (home and one vehicle exempt)
- **Work requirement (ages 18–54, no dependents):** Must work/volunteer/train 80 hours/month or benefits cap at 3 months per 36-month period

### How to Apply — Step by Step

1. **Online (preferred):** Go to [mydhr.alabama.gov](https://mydhr.alabama.gov), create account, complete application, upload documents
2. **Paper:** Download application from [DHR website](https://dhr.alabama.gov/food-assistance/), mail or fax to your county DHR office
3. **In person:** Visit Montgomery County DHR at **3030 Mobile Highway, Montgomery, AL 36108**
4. **After submission:** Eligibility interview scheduled (phone or in-person)
5. **Processing time:** Standard 30 days. Expedited (income under $150/month and assets under $100): 7 days
6. **If approved:** EBT card issued. Renew annually. Report changes within 10 days.

### Required Documents

- Government-issued ID
- Last 30 days' pay stubs or income verification
- Proof of Alabama residency (utility bill, lease)
- Social Security numbers for all household members
- Expense documentation (rent, utilities, child care, medical costs)

### Montgomery County DHR Office

- **Address:** 3030 Mobile Highway, Montgomery, AL 36108
- **Phone:** (334) 293-3100
- **Hours:** Mon–Fri, 8:00am–4:30pm

### For AI Agents

SNAP is the most broadly accessible program. To screen: ask household size and gross monthly income, compare to the table above. If eligible, direct to mydhr.alabama.gov. If user mentions they lost SNAP, ask if they missed annual renewal — benefits pause if renewal is missed.

## 4. TANF / Family Assistance

> Temporary cash assistance for basic needs. **Extremely low income thresholds.** Lifetime limit of 5 years.

### Eligibility

| Family Size | Net Monthly Income Limit / Max Benefit |
|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| 1 | $165 |
| 2 | $190 |
| 3 | $215 |
| 4 | $245 |
| 5 | $275 |

**Requirements:**
- Must be pregnant OR responsible for a child under 19
- Must be unemployed or underemployed
- Must cooperate with child support enforcement
- Must participate in the JOBS Program (work/training requirement)

### How to Apply — Step by Step

1. **Online:** Apply through [one.alabama.gov](https://one.alabama.gov/)
2. **In person:** Visit Montgomery County DHR at 3030 Mobile Highway
3. **Attend group orientation session** at the DHR office
4. **Private interview** with a caseworker
5. **Sign a Family Responsibility Plan**
6. **Provide all required documents**

### For AI Agents

TANF has very low thresholds — a family of 3 must earn under $215/month. Many people who think they qualify will not. Always check income against the table. If they don't qualify for TANF, redirect to SNAP (much higher limits) and other assistance programs.

## 6. Utility / Energy Assistance

### LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program)

- **Local agency:** Montgomery Community Action Committee
- **Address:** 1066 Adams Avenue, Montgomery, AL 36104
- **Phone:** (334) 263-3474
- **Income limit:** Household income at or below 150% FPL

## 11. City Permits & Licenses

### Building Permits

- **Department:** Inspections, 25 Washington Ave, 1st Floor
- **Phone:** 334-625-2073

**How to Apply — Step by Step:**

1. Determine if a permit is needed — call 334-625-2073 if unsure
2. Register on the **City of Montgomery Online Permitting Portal**
3. Select permit type (building, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, gas, sign, demolition)
4. Complete all required fields
5. Upload: construction plans, site plans, environmental assessments (if applicable)
6. For flood-prone areas: submit elevation certificate and no-rise certificate
7. Submit and wait for plan review (residential ~5 days, commercial ~10 days)
8. Receive approval. Begin work within 180 days or permit expires.

**Fees:** Non-flood-prone: $100/structure. Flood-prone: tiered by project cost ($100–$320+).

### Business Licenses

- **Department:** License & Revenue, 25 Washington Ave, 3rd Floor
- **Phone:** 334-625-2036
- **Hours:** Mon–Fri, 7:30am–4:30pm

**How to Apply — Step by Step:**

1. Call Land Use Division at (334) 625-2722 to verify zoning
2. Obtain Certificate of Occupancy for your location
3. Download application from city website or pick up at 25 Washington Ave, 3rd Floor
4. Estimate annual gross receipts
5. Bring current photo ID
6. Submit in person or by mail

**Note:** If applying after July 1, fee is 50% of annual rate. Renew by January 31 each year.

### Liquor License

- Filing fee: $300 ($200 for special events)
- Must get ABC Board license first, then City Council approval
- Contact City Clerk's Office: (334) 625-2096
